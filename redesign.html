Responsive Redesign

Rationale:

I interned at this company and kept finding this website difficult to navigate because of its lack of affordances. As a digital marketing company, this website does a poor job of attracting customers. Link to original website.

View Redesigned Page!

Usability Issues

a.
Efficiency:
This company’s product is its branding strategy which appears last in the list under the Services tab. Getting to this page needs the user to mouse over the navbar which may not be intuitive to users.
b.
Memorability:
Not too bad but user gets frontloaded with way too much content before they get an “action item”. May cause them to forget their task at hand.
c.
Learnability:
The main content of their Services list is not alphabetized/in any specific order. It’s confusing to look for a particular service and therefore learn the interface.
d.
Hierarchy:
There are no clear markers or guided flow for the user. Unclear about what is prioritized.
e.
Color:
Too many different shades of orange, beige, and gray are used. The importance value and contrast decreases
f.
Unclear goal and audience:
The credibility of the company is buried down which would be a main attraction to a lot of customers. A lot of their main content gets missed because of lack of clarity.
g.
Hindrances:
The mouse-over menu can be distracting and pops up even when not required.

Accessibility Issues

A. The WAVE tool shows that the lack of contrast between the subheadings and background can be difficult to access for people with low vision / colour blindness.
B. There are no visual indicators showing how the Tab space is used for navigation. People not using a mouse would have a very difficult time going through this website.
C. Actions such as mouse-overs that for their main content renders this website further inaccessible to those using screen readers
D. There are some basic issues such as low contrast, and lack of alt text that WAVE has caught. But it doesn’t detect larger issues such as unclear navigation by keyboard.

Reflection

  • What's better now?
    • Usability: Clearer Call to Action button
    • Accessibility: Much easier and clearer tab navigation. Removed the inaccessible hover interaction
    • Hierarchy: More defined through colour and heading levels
    • Efficiency: More affordances through nav bars and specific buttons guiding user flow
  • Learnings
    • Learnt how to walk through the specific steps from website critique to implementation
    • Learnt how to use media queries and collapses
    • Learnt how to simplify messages and create hierarchies
    • Spent a lot of time making sure things are very very precise. I learnt that that's a tradeoff against having more functionality
