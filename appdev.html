    Key Learnings

    A. Team Work: We were a group of 4 students working remotely and held meetings twice a week to coordinate and communicate. There were a lot of times when we were deadlocked and had opposing viewpoints, but this project taught me how to navigate that
    B. Iteration: Design processes are never final. As the interface designer, I had to present my designs multiple times and ask for feedback from different sets of people. I learnt the importance of not getting married to a particular way of doing things.
    C. Organisation: We had a very quick turnaround time (2 months) within which we had to follow every single step from creating user scenarios to presenting the final product. This project taught me how to create deadlines and assign roles to keep team mates accountable.

    App Progress

    This app is still being published, but our extensive user research brought up a lot of questions about the pressure students applying to colleges go through, the threats posed by anonymous postings, data ethics, and the disconnect between what students think the colleges want and what the admissions department actually might look for.
