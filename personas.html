Personas & Storyboard

Rationale:

This was my introductory experience into understanding the behaviours of users through interviewing, observing and actually understanding their problems with the app GoodNotes.

Choice of Interface:

GoodNotes:
This is a note-taking application that a lot of students using IPads seem to like a lot. It digitally simulates taking notes in a physical notebook and allows students to organize their content in one place. Most often students use an Apple pencil as a stylus to use this application. Structures of GoodNotes

GoodNotes Analysis

Problems solved:

  • The inability to handwrite digital notes in an organized manner for academic purposes.
  • Physical notebooks take up too much space, can be a burden to carry around, are difficult to search through.
  • Writing notes on Apple notes makes it difficult to organize and streams away from the folder structure of desktops that students are very used to.
  • Searchability is low and time-consuming

    • Key components:

  • Page layout is reminiscent of a physical notebook with templates such as the yellow pages (pictured above)
  • User can handwrite notes and search through them using the search button. Handwriting recognition on this app is pretty strong
  • The interface lets the user rearrange content within a matter of very few clicks
  • Files can be organized in a folder structure similar to that on a desktop

    Key Observations

    A. While reading, user scrolls down with finger of the same hand that they hold the pencil in. Doesn’t use their non-dominant hand.
    B. User flips over to landscape mode to write for longer period of time
    C. They load downloaded slides into the app and annotate it
    D. The iPad continues to remain on even when user is not actively using the app. Idle time causes battery wastage. ​
    E. User scribbles extra notes in the margin and shifts content around pretty frequently using the lasso tool​ ​

    Persona Analysis:

    Quick Quentin:

  • Is a student who is overwhelmed by the number of tasks he has to complete and tries to find ways of optimizing his time whenever he can.
  • His problem is that he scribbles notes too quickly and so the searchability of his handwriting decreases.
  • He also hates that he has to switch between his laptop and iPad because GoodNotes doesn’t have a convenient typing interface.
  • My interviews have showed that a lot of users depend on GoodNotes for its quick note-taking ability.

    • Tangible Terry:

  • Is the user who likes to write down notes to understand content. She annotates her notes extensively and draws a lot of diagrams for her own clarity.
  • She’s the organized student whose GOAL is to create and have learning aids in one place to do well in academics.
  • Terry's problem is that she constantly has the app open during lecture because she might need to jot something down at any point of time.
  • A lot of users talk about how useful GoodNotes is for them to draw diagrams quickly for reinforcing their learning.
